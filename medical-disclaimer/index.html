Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: July 17, 2026

The short version I'm not a doctor. Nothing on this site is medical advice, a diagnosis, or a treatment plan, and this site is not affiliated with, reviewed by, or endorsed by any doctor, podiatrist, or medical practice.

Who I am, and who I'm not

My name is Arshak Nersisyan. I run Flat Feet Shoe Guide as one person, not a clinic or a medical publication. I have grade 2 flat feet myself, and everything I write comes from my own first-hand experience shopping for and wearing shoes and insoles — not from clinical training. I am not a doctor, podiatrist, physical therapist, orthotist, or any other kind of licensed medical or healthcare professional, and I don't have medical credentials of any kind related to feet, footwear, or orthopedics.

This site has no medical affiliation

Flat Feet Shoe Guide is not affiliated with, sponsored by, reviewed by, or endorsed by any doctor, podiatrist, physical therapist, hospital, clinic, or medical organization. Any mention of a "podiatrist" or "physician" on this site is a general recommendation to seek professional care, not a reference to a specific person or practice associated with this site.

What this site is (and isn't) for

This site shares personal opinions and general buying guidance about footwear, insoles, and comfort for people with flat feet — things like how a shoe felt after a few months of wear, or what to look for in an insole. It is not:

  • A diagnosis of flat feet, plantar fasciitis, overpronation, or any other foot or health condition
  • A treatment plan, exercise program, or medical recommendation tailored to your specific feet
  • A substitute for an in-person examination by a licensed podiatrist or physician
  • Guaranteed to apply to your situation — my own experience with grade 2 flat feet does not mean a product or piece of advice will work the same way for your feet

When to see an actual doctor

Please see a podiatrist or physician, rather than relying on this site, if you experience:

  • Persistent or worsening pain in your feet, ankles, knees, hips, or lower back
  • Sudden changes in the shape of your foot or arch
  • Numbness, tingling, swelling, or discoloration that doesn't resolve
  • Pain that doesn't improve after a reasonable trial of more supportive footwear
  • Any symptom that concerns you, regardless of whether it's listed here

Delaying proper medical care because of something you read on this site is never the right call. When in doubt, talk to a real, licensed professional.

No guarantees

Reviews, comparisons, and buying guides on this site reflect my own experience and opinion at the time of writing. Products, availability, and pricing can change, and what worked for my feet may not work for yours. Use your own judgment, and check the manufacturer's current information before buying anything.
