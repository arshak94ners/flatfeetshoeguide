Frequently asked questions

What order should I read these guides in?

Start with understanding your feet (the at-home test and the flat feet vs. plantar fasciitis vs. overpronation guide), then move to terminology if shoe descriptions feel confusing, then use the tools once you know what you're looking for. If you already know what hurts, start with the foot pain diagnosis guide instead.

Are these guides a substitute for seeing a podiatrist?

No. Everything here is written from personal experience with flat feet, not medical training. These guides explain common mechanical patterns and terminology, not diagnoses or treatment. See our medical disclaimer for the full distinction.

How often are new guides added?

We add guides as real gaps come up, rather than on a fixed schedule. If there's a specific topic you'd like covered, our contact page is the fastest way to request it.
