A walking shoe, not a converted runner

Most shoes recommended for flat feet — the Adrenaline GTS, Gel-Kayano, even the 990 — are running or lifestyle shoes pressed into service for walking and standing. The 928 is different: New Balance built it specifically as a walking shoe, with a firmer, less flexible sole designed around walking gait mechanics rather than a running stride. That distinction matters because walking and running put stress through the foot differently, and a shoe optimized for one isn't automatically optimal for the other.

The rollbar heel stabilizer

The 928's defining feature is its rollbar heel stabilizer — a firm plastic structure built into the heel counter that resists excess side-to-side motion during the heel-strike phase of a walking stride. For flat feet, where the heel and ankle often roll inward more than they should, this is a targeted piece of engineering rather than generic cushioning.

Function over fashion Buyer feedback consistently describes the 928 as looking more "orthopedic" than a typical lifestyle sneaker. That's a fair trade-off for many people prioritizing stability over style, but worth knowing before you buy if appearance matters to you.

Width and fit

Like the 990 series, New Balance sells the 928 across an unusually broad width range, which matters given how often flat feet come with a wider forefoot. This combination of true walking-shoe design and wide sizing is the core reason it holds up as a recommendation.

Where it falls short

  • Aesthetics. Looks noticeably more clinical than casual lifestyle sneakers.
  • Cushioning. Less plush than max-cushion walking shoes like the Hoka Bondi.
  • Limited colorways. Fewer color options than mainstream sneaker lines.

Who this shoe is right for

Good fit

Anyone prioritizing genuine walking-shoe stability and width over lifestyle-sneaker looks.

Look elsewhere if

You want maximum cushioning for hard-floor standing — see our Hoka Bondi review instead.

Bottom line

The 928 isn't trying to be a fashion statement, and buyers who choose it generally aren't looking for one. As a purpose-built walking shoe with genuine stability engineering and real width options, it holds up as a strong, if plain-looking, choice for flat feet.

Frequently asked questions

Is the New Balance 928 good for standing all day?

Yes, its firm, wide base and rollbar heel stabilizer are specifically designed for the kind of stability that helps with long periods of standing or walking on hard floors.

Is the 928 a running shoe?

No, it's purpose-built as a walking shoe, not a running shoe. Its firmer, less flexible sole is designed for walking mechanics rather than running gait.
