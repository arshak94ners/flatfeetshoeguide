Superfeet GREEN vs. Powerstep Pinnacle: Which Insole?

Research by Arshak Nersisyan · Published July 18, 2026 · 5 min read

Both show up constantly in insole recommendations for flat feet, and both appear on our insoles guide. The real difference between them is firmness — here's how to tell which level of structure actually fits your feet.

Firmness: the core difference

The GREEN's rigid biomechanical shell is built specifically to resist significant arch collapse, holding the heel in a more neutral position rather than just cushioning the area. The Pinnacle takes a softer approach — a semi-rigid shell under a cushioned top layer, aiming for noticeable support without the fully rigid feel. Neither is objectively "better"; they're matched to different degrees of arch collapse.

Who tends to prefer each

Based on published specs and aggregated buyer feedback, the GREEN is consistently recommended for people who've already tried softer insoles and found them insufficient. The Pinnacle shows up more often as a first insole purchase, or for people who found fully rigid orthotics uncomfortable rather than supportive.

Fit and shoe volume

Both are trim-to-fit, but the GREEN's rigid shell takes up more volume inside a shoe than the Pinnacle's semi-rigid construction. If you're already wearing snug-fitting shoes, the Pinnacle is less likely to crowd your fit.

Durability

The GREEN's rigid shell is built to hold its shape longer than the Pinnacle's softer cushioning layer, which will compress and need replacing somewhat sooner under daily use.

Our verdict

Choose Superfeet GREEN if you have noticeable, significant arch collapse and need real structural correction. Choose the Powerstep Pinnacle if you want meaningfully more support than a stock insole without a fully rigid feel, or if you're not sure yet how much structure you need.

Frequently asked questions

Which insole is firmer: Superfeet GREEN or Powerstep Pinnacle?

The Superfeet GREEN is noticeably firmer, built around a rigid stabilizer shell for significant arch collapse. The Powerstep Pinnacle uses a semi-rigid shell for a more moderate, comfort-balanced level of support.

Which insole should a first-time buyer choose?

If you've never used a supportive insole before and aren't sure how much structure you need, the Powerstep Pinnacle's moderate support is a gentler starting point. If you already know your arch has collapsed significantly, the Superfeet GREEN is the more targeted choice.
