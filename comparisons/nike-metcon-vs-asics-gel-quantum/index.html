Both appear on our gym shoe guide, but they're built for different sessions — one for barbell stability, one for a mix of cardio and moderate lifting. Here's how to tell which fits your routine.

The core trade-off: stability vs. cushioning

The Metcon commits fully to lifting stability — a firm, nearly flat heel plate that minimizes compression under a loaded bar. That firmness comes at a real cost for cardio: buyer feedback consistently describes the Metcon as uncomfortable over sustained running or jump-rope work. The Gel-Quantum splits the difference, pairing more forgiving cushioning with a still-stable heel structure, at the cost of some stability under maximal loads.

Matching the shoe to your actual sessions

If your gym time is mostly heavy barbell work — squats, deadlifts, presses — the Metcon's stability advantage is worth its firmer ride. If your sessions mix cardio, HIIT circuits, and moderate strength work, the Gel-Quantum's added cushioning will likely feel better across a full hour-long class.

Fit

Both are available in standard and wide widths, which matters for the wider forefoot that often comes with flat feet. Neither offers as broad a width range as dedicated walking shoes, but both accommodate flat feet reasonably well within their category.

Durability

Nike builds the Metcon specifically to withstand rope climbs and barbell drops, and it's rated highly for durability under that kind of abuse. The Gel-Quantum isn't built with the same reinforcement for repeated heavy barbell contact, though it holds up fine under typical mixed-training use.

Our verdict

Choose the Nike Metcon if your training is mostly heavy lifting and you need maximum ground stability. Choose the ASICS Gel-Quantum if your sessions mix cardio and moderate strength work and comfort across a full hour matters more than maximal barbell stability.

Frequently asked questions

Which is better for lifting: Metcon or Gel-Quantum?

The Nike Metcon is built specifically for lifting stability, with a firm, near-flat heel plate. The ASICS Gel-Quantum is more cushioned and better suited to mixed cardio and moderate lifting rather than maximal barbell work.

Can I use either shoe for running on a treadmill between sets?

The Gel-Quantum's added cushioning makes it more comfortable for short cardio bursts. The Metcon's firm heel is specifically designed for lifting stability and tends to feel harsh over sustained running.
